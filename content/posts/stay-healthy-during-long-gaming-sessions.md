You're going to be sitting in front of a screen for a long time. That's the whole point. But there's a difference between having a great time and leaving the venue feeling like you've been hit by a bus.

## Drink water

This is the number one thing. Not energy drinks, not just coffee. Actual water. Keep a bottle at your desk and refill it. You'll feel better, play better, and not get a headache at 2 AM.

Cold drinks and beer are available at cost, but they don't replace water.

## Eat something real

Snacks are fine for keeping the energy up between meals, but at some point you need actual food. If people are organizing a group order, get in on it. Don't just run on chips and sugar for 16 hours.

## Get up

Your body was not designed to sit in a gaming chair for an entire weekend. Stand up, stretch, walk around. Every hour or so is ideal. Your back, neck, and wrists will thank you the next day.

Quick stretch routine:
- Roll your shoulders back
- Stretch your wrists and fingers
- Stand up and touch your toes (or try)
- Look at something far away for 30 seconds

Takes two minutes. Makes a real difference.

## Sleep

You can sleep wherever you find a spot, but remember that the venue stays active. If you're the type who can nap through noise, great. If not, the city isn't far for a proper rest.

Pushing through exhaustion past a certain point stops being fun and starts being miserable. Know your limit.

## Mental reset

Lost a rough match? Frustrated with your team? Step away for a few minutes. Walk outside, get some air, cool down. Coming back tilted just makes the next game worse too.

Nobody expects you to be on peak form for every minute of the event. Pace yourself.
