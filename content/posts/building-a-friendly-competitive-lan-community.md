The whole reason we're here is to play games together. Some of that is casual, some of it is competitive, and all of it works better when people aren't terrible to each other.

## Competitive doesn't mean toxic

Play hard, try to win, trash-talk if it's mutual and everyone's laughing. But know the line. If someone's not having fun with it, dial it back. Winning a match isn't worth making someone's weekend worse.

## Help the new people

Not everyone at the LAN has done this before. Some people don't know the games, the setup, or the culture. Be the person who helps them figure it out instead of the person who makes them feel stupid for not knowing.

Small things matter:
- Show someone how to join the server
- Invite them into a casual round
- Explain what's happening in a tournament

That's how the community grows.

## Suggest games

The game list isn't locked. If you want to play something, bring it up on Discord in #general or just rally people at the venue. If it works, we'll make it happen. If you and your friends want to do your own thing on the side, go for it.

## When competition gets heated

It happens. Someone makes a bad call, a match feels unfair, frustration builds up. When it does:

1. Take a breath before you say something you'll regret.
2. If it's a real issue, talk to an organizer. They'll sort it out.
3. Don't take it to public chat or make it everyone's problem.

Most of these situations blow over in five minutes if people handle them calmly.

## The real experience

The best LAN memories aren't usually about winning. They're about the ridiculous plays, the unexpected comebacks, and the people you were with when it happened. Keep that in mind when the scoreboard doesn't go your way.
