A LAN party is a social event. The games are the reason we're here, but the people are what make it worth coming back.

## House rules

1. **Arrival and departure** — Be on time. Bring and take away your own equipment.
2. **Equipment** — Bring your own PC, cables, and headset. **Speakers are prohibited.**
3. **Network** — Do not change network settings or swap cables.
4. **While gaming** — Respect others: no cheating, no deliberate disruption.
5. **Cleanliness** — Eat and drink only in designated areas. No crumbs at the rigs.
6. **Security** — You are responsible for your own gear. Do not touch other people's machines.
7. **Atmosphere** — The goal is shared gaming and fun. Be fair and be cool.

## Be decent

This should go without saying, but:

- Treat everyone with respect, in person and in voice chat.
- Don't trash-talk beyond what's clearly playful and mutual.
- No harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks. Period.
- Respect other people's space, gear, and boundaries.

If someone asks you to stop, stop.

## Welcome new people

Not everyone has been to a LAN before. Some people might feel out of place at first. A small thing like explaining how something works, inviting someone into a warm-up match, or just saying hi goes a long way.

Nobody should feel like they don't belong here.

## Take care of yourself

Long sessions are part of the fun, but don't run yourself into the ground.

- Drink water. Seriously.
- Eat real food at some point — in the designated area.
- Get up and stretch. Your back will thank you tomorrow.
- If you're tired, take a break. Sleep if you need to. The games will still be there.

## Take care of each other

If someone near you seems off — exhausted, upset, or unwell — check in. You don't have to fix anything, just asking "you good?" matters.

If someone needs help they can't get from a friend, point them to an organizer.

## When things go wrong

If there's a conflict:

1. Step back. Don't escalate.
2. Talk to an organizer.
3. We'll handle it quickly and privately.

We take this seriously. Anyone making the event worse for others will be asked to change their behavior or leave.

## The short version

Be the kind of person you'd want to sit next to for a whole weekend. That's it.
